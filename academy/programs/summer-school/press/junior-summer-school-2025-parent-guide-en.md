# Junior Summer School 2025 - Parent Guide

## Program Overview

The Junior Summer School 2025 is a two-week technology program designed specifically for teenagers aged 15-18. Our curriculum focuses on digital literacy, online safety, and creative technology skills in a fun, supportive environment.

**Dates:** July 7 - July 21, 2025  
**Location:** Private Campus, Craiova  
**Hours:** 9:00 AM - 4:00 PM, Monday through Friday

## What Your Teen Will Learn

### Digital Literacy & Online Safety
- Introduction to Unix/Linux command line basics through games and interactive activities
- Social media privacy, security settings, and recognizing online risks
- Understanding AI technologies, their benefits and potential concerns
- Cyber hygiene practices including password security and phishing awareness

### Creative Technology
- Open-source multimedia tools for content creation
- Complete podcast production process (planning, recording, editing, publishing)
- Digital storytelling techniques
- Collaborative technology projects

## Daily Schedule

- **8:30 - 9:00 AM:** Drop-off and morning social time

- **10:30 - 10:45 AM:** Break
- **10:45 - 12:15 PM:** Hands-on workshop
- **12:15 - 1:00 PM:** Lunch (provided)

- **2:30 - 2:45 PM:** Break

## What to Bring

- Laptop (if available - we can provide one if needed)
- Headphones or earbuds
- Water bottle
- Notebook and pen/pencil
- Enthusiasm and creativity!

## Staff & Supervision

All instructors are experienced educators with expertise in technology and working with teenagers. Our staff-to-student ratio is 1:10, ensuring personalized attention and proper supervision. All staff members have undergone background checks and safety training.

## Safety Protocols

- Campus is secured with controlled access points
- All participants must be signed in and out each day
- Internet access is monitored and filtered for age-appropriate content
- Emergency procedures are in place and regularly practiced
- First aid personnel available on-site at all times

## Communication

Parents will receive:
- Pre-program orientation materials one week before start date
- Daily update emails with highlights and upcoming activities
- Access to a secure photo-sharing platform to see activities in progress
- Staff contact information for any questions or concerns
- Invitation to the final project showcase on July 21st

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is prior technology experience required?
No, the program is designed for beginners with no previous technical experience needed.

### How are participants grouped?
Students are placed in small teams of 5-6 with peers of similar age. These teams collaborate on projects while receiving individualized instruction.

### What if my teen needs to miss a day?
Please notify us in advance. We'll provide materials to help them catch up, and staff will assist them upon return.

### Is there a dress code?
Comfortable, casual clothing suitable for a classroom environment. We recommend layers as indoor temperatures may vary.

### Can parents visit during the program?
We have a designated visitor day midway through the program (July 14th), and all parents are invited to the final showcase on July 21st.

### What COVID-19 protocols are in place?
We follow current health authority guidelines and will provide specific protocols closer to the program date.
